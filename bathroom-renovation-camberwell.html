Regulatory Compliance in Victoria

Compliance with state legislation is non-negotiable for wet-area upgrades. Any project valued over $10,000 must be undertaken by a registered building practitioner to ensure legal and safety standards are met. Furthermore, if the total work exceeds $16,000, the builder must provide Domestic Building Insurance, which protects the homeowner if the contractor cannot complete the job.

Waterproofing represents a critical technical component. All waterproofing applications must meet the Australian Standard AS 3740:2021. Failures in this area are a common source of defects, so adherence to this standard is essential for protecting the home's structure. Permits are generally required for structural changes, relocating wet areas, and new plumbing installations. Cosmetic updates that do not alter the existing layout, such as painting or replacing fixtures in the same position, may not require a permit, but it is always best to confirm with the relevant authorities.

Working with Period Architecture

Camberwell is characterised by a significant number of Federation and Edwardian homes. Renovating these properties requires a specific approach that respects the original character while incorporating modern amenities. Heritage-sympathetic design often involves selecting fixtures and tile profiles that complement the era's aesthetic, rather than clashing with it.

Practical challenges in these older buildings include lath-and-plaster walls and narrow service runs. Consequently, budgets for these homes should account for the unknowns often revealed once demolition begins, such as the need to relocate plumbing to align with updated layouts. Allowing extra time for these complexities is essential. Heritage-sympathetic designs might include custom joinery to fit awkward alcoves or specific tile selections that mirror the home's era.

Phases of the Renovation Process

A successful renovation follows a logical sequence of events to ensure efficiency and quality. The process typically begins with a consultation to define the scope, followed by the design phase where materials and layouts are finalised. Once a fixed-price quote is accepted, any necessary building permits are arranged before work begins on site.

The construction phase starts with demolition and the rough-in of plumbing and electrical services. This is followed by waterproofing, which must be allowed to cure properly. Tiling and the installation of joinery, such as vanities, come next. Finally, the fit-off of tapware, lighting, and accessories occurs, leading to a final inspection and handover. Understanding these steps helps homeowners monitor progress effectively.

Common questions

Do I need a permit for a bathroom renovation? A permit is required if the work involves structural changes, relocating wet areas, or new plumbing installations. Cosmetic updates that do not alter the layout generally do not require a permit.

What is the typical timeline? The construction phase usually takes 2 to 4 weeks. Additional time, often 2 to 6 weeks, should be allowed for planning, design, and permit approvals before work begins.

How much does labour cost? Labour typically accounts for 40 to 60 per cent of the total renovation cost, with the remainder covering materials and fixtures.
